Mileage Fraud and Counterfeit Parts – A Silent Epidemic

Mileage Fraud: The Hidden Deception

Each year, millions of used vehicles are sold globally with falsified mileage, creating a widespread issue that affects the economy, driver safety, and the environment.

  • A Universal Problem: In Europe, 15.2% of used cars have altered mileage, according to carVertical (2023). In Germany, this figure reaches nearly 20%, causing average losses of €4,000 to €6,000 per vehicle. In the United States, 450,000 cars are sold annually with manipulated odometers, resulting in a total loss of $1 billion for buyers. (carVertical, 2023, Carfax, 2024)
  • Colossal Financial Impact: In Europe alone, mileage fraud costs consumers nearly €9.6 billion annually. Beyond the purchase price, unsuspecting buyers face unexpected repair costs and breakdowns. (UFC-Que Choisir, 2023)
  • Hidden Safety Risks: Driving a vehicle with falsified mileage significantly increases risks. Worn brakes, aging suspensions, or engines nearing failure pose a direct threat to road safety.

Counterfeit Parts: A Growing Danger

  • A $45 Billion Black Market: Approximately 15% of all automotive parts globally are counterfeit, according to the World Customs Organization (2023). These parts mimic originals but often fail basic safety tests. (WCO, 2023)
  • Shocking Statistics: An AAA (2023) study revealed that 85% of counterfeit brake pads fail to meet minimum safety standards. In some cases, these parts double braking distances, significantly increasing the risk of accidents. (AAA, 2023)
  • Massive Seizures: In 2022, Mercedes-Benz conducted 500 raids across 60 countries, confiscating 1.7 million counterfeit parts. Despite these efforts, the problem continues to grow. (Daimler AG, 2023)

Real Consequences for Everyone

  • For Consumers: Buying a car with falsified mileage or counterfeit parts can cost thousands in repairs and compromise passenger safety.
  • For the Industry: Counterfeiting robs legitimate manufacturers of billions annually, stifling innovation and driving up costs for genuine products.
  • For Society: In South Africa, 20% of serious accidents are linked to counterfeit parts. Similar issues are underreported in other regions. (NAAMSA, 2023)

    • Real mileage, logged automatically and securely.

    • Verified data for critical parts to ensure authenticity.
  2. An Immutable Public Registry: Blockchain acts as a digital notary, recording every detail in an open, tamper-proof ledger. This eliminates fraud and restores trust between buyers and sellers.
  3. Certification for Automotive Parts: NFTs for critical parts allow buyers to confirm their authenticity and compliance with safety standards instantly.
  4. Immediate Impact:
    • For Buyers: Transparent vehicle data ensures informed decisions.
    • For the Industry: Improved traceability protects manufacturers and fosters consumer trust.

Point 2: Vehicle Traceability – Revolutionizing Transparency in the Automotive Industry

The lack of transparency: A systemic issue

The automotive industry has long suffered from a lack of effective vehicle traceability. Buyers, insurers, and even governments often struggle to access reliable data about a vehicle's history, creating a climate of distrust and fueling fraudulent activities.

  1. Incomplete or falsified histories: Existing databases, often fragmented by country or manufacturer, leave significant gaps in information. Approximately 25% of used vehicles sold globally show discrepancies in their history, according to a 2023 McKinsey study. (McKinsey & Co., 2023)
  2. Significant economic losses: The lack of traceability costs the global automotive industry around $60 billion annually, through fraud or additional expenses to reconstruct reliable histories. (Allianz Partners, 2024)
  3. A barrier to a circular economy: Traceability is essential to promote the reuse of parts and recycling of vehicles, a priority aligned with global climate objectives.

Current technology: An imperfect solution

  • Lack of international standardization: Each country or manufacturer uses its own systems, making a unified overview impossible.
  • Data that isn’t immutable: Existing databases can be altered or hacked, undermining their reliability.
  • High costs: Services like Carfax charge significant fees for reports that are often incomplete.

Carify: Ushering in a new era of automotive traceability

  1. A unique NFT for every vehicle: Each car is associated with a unique NFT containing all critical data: mileage, repair history, accidents, and ownership records. This NFT serves as a trustworthy "digital identity card," accessible anytime.
  2. A global, immutable ledger: Unlike traditional databases, blockchain-stored information is tamper-proof. Every update is timestamped and verified by the network, eliminating fraud.
  3. Universal accessibility: Buyers, dealers, and insurers can access a vehicle’s full history by scanning a QR code or using a dedicated app.

A positive impact for all stakeholders

  • For buyers: You can purchase a vehicle with confidence, knowing its data is complete and reliable.
  • For dealers and manufacturers: Better traceability enhances consumer trust and reduces disputes. It also opens up opportunities for premium services like certified used vehicle programs.
  • For governments: Enhanced transparency curtails tax fraud and simplifies the enforcement of environmental regulations.
  • For the environment: Effective traceability encourages part reuse and recycling, fostering a more sustainable industry.

Economic and societal impact

According to a 2024 Deloitte analysis, a solution like Carify’s could:

  • Reduce fraud by 80%, saving billions annually.
  • Boost dealer revenues through blockchain-based certification programs.
  • Lower CO₂ emissions tied to new part production by increasing recycling.

At Carify, we believe trust is essential in every automotive transaction. By leveraging blockchain for traceability, we provide consumers, professionals, and governments with a powerful tool to create a safer, fairer, and more sustainable marketplace.
